> "Health is a state of complete physical, mental and social wellbeing and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity."

We all know this definition. But how many of us actually treat health as a whole?  
In real life, one part always gets ignored when things get busy. So why is our health data still just a scattered collection of independent data points that give us no meaningful picture of **Health** itself?

### Why I Care

My journey began in physiotherapy and sports science. Day after day, I saw how fragmented and inconsistent health data creates real problems for patients, athletes, and clinicians. A blood pressure reading from six months ago, a week of heart rate data, a casual "How do you feel today?" in consultation, everything lives in isolation. We overvalue some numbers and undervalue others. Nothing connects.

This frustration became the premise of my non-linear path.

What started as a simple blog on a no-code platform slowly evolved as I grew more comfortable building. Today, it has become **SportYNE** - a multi-storage, deterministic-stochastic pipeline that tries to do justice to both the subjective nature of human language and objective clinical frameworks.

### SportYNE - Your Next Evolution

SportYNE is a digital health pipeline that respects how people actually speak and write about their health while grounding everything in structured frameworks like the **ICF (International Classification of Functioning)**.

It processes messy clinical notes, resolves pronouns, tracks people across conversations, structures information, and maintains consistency, especially when the data comes from multiple turns and sources.
